About the project

A transfer-learning screening aid for retinal disease

RetinaAI is a research and portfolio project applying deep convolutional transfer learning to retinal fundus photography. It is built to be retrained on any folder-structured dataset without touching the codebase, and to be transparent about its own confidence and reasoning via Grad-CAM attention maps.

Architecture

The classifier uses transfer learning from an accuracy-first ImageNet backbone (EfficientNetB7 by default, with automatic fallback to EfficientNetV2L, DenseNet201, or ResNet152V2 if the preferred backbone cannot be loaded), followed by a custom classification head with batch normalization and dropout for regularization.

Preprocessing

Every image is validated, center-cropped, resized with aspect-ratio preservation, denoised, contrast enhanced with CLAHE, and sharpened — identically at both training and inference time.
